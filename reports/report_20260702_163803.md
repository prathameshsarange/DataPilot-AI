**Common Mistakes:**
*   **Lack of Specificity:** Answering questions vaguely without concrete examples from projects.
*   **Not Explaining "Why":** For technical questions, simply stating "what" something is without explaining "why" it's used or its benefits.
*   **Poor Code Walkthrough:** Struggling to articulate your thought process when asked to solve a coding problem.
*   **Neglecting Soft Skills:** Focusing solely on technical prowess and failing to demonstrate teamwork, communication, or problem-solving.
*   **No Questions for Interviewer:** Not preparing insightful questions to ask, indicating a lack of genuine interest.

**Interview Tips:**
*   **Master Your Projects:** Be ready to talk in detail about every aspect of your projects, including challenges, design choices, and learning outcomes.
*   **Practice Explaining:** Don't just know the answers; practice explaining complex technical concepts clearly and concisely.
*   **Whiteboard Practice:** Practice solving coding problems on a whiteboard or online collaborative editor while vocalizing your thought process.
*   **Research the Company:** Understand their tech stack, products, and culture. Tailor your answers to align with their values.
*   **Prepare Questions:** Have 3-5 thoughtful questions ready to ask the interviewer about the role, team, or company.
*   **Follow the STAR Method:** For behavioral questions, structure your answers using Situation, Task, Action, and Result.
*   **Show Enthusiasm:** Your passion for learning and technology should be evident.
*   **Review Fundamentals:** Even with advanced skills, sometimes interviewers test basic CS concepts (data structures, algorithms, OS).
